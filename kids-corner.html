Grandma Moves In

A warm story about big changes, big feelings, and even bigger love.

1
Pixel and Dot were playing in the living room when Mom called from the hallway.

"Good news, you two! Grandma Rosie is coming to live with us - starting tomorrow!"
2
Dot jumped up and spun around. "Grandma's moving IN? Like, forever?!"

Pixel went quiet. Forever felt like a very long word.
3
That night, Pixel whispered to Dot in the dark.

"What if she takes our playroom? What if she doesn't like our cartoons?"

Dot thought about it. "What if she loves our cartoons?"
4
Grandma Rosie arrived with two big suitcases, a rocking chair, and a tin of cookies shaped like stars.

She smelled like lavender and warm bread, and her hugs lasted exactly long enough.
5
She didn't take the playroom. She got the sunny corner room at the end of the hall.

She did ask about the cartoons, though - and she had a lot of opinions.
6
Some days were still tricky. Grandma Rosie liked the TV volume loud.

Pixel liked quiet. Dot liked everything loud. So at least two of them were happy.
7
One rainy afternoon, Grandma Rosie taught Pixel how to fold paper cranes.

"Every fold is a wish," she said. "So fold carefully."

Pixel folded twelve cranes. Twelve wishes, just in case.
8
Dot ate four of the star cookies without asking.

Grandma Rosie didn't mind. She just made more.

"That's what cookie tins are for," she said with a wink.
9
By the end of the first week, Grandma Rosie's rocking chair had a special spot by the window.

And Pixel and Dot had a special spot - one on each armrest - whenever they needed a story.
10
That night, Pixel whispered to Dot again.

"I think I was wrong about forever."

Dot smiled sleepily. "I think forever smells like lavender and cookies."

The End

Home grows a little bigger every time someone new fills it with love.
